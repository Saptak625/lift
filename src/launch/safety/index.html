FAQ: Concerns and Dangers of Space Travel



Concern 1: Travel without Gravity

The absence of gravity in space can cause a variety of problems for humans, including muscle and bone loss, and other physiological changes. It can also make it difficult to perform tasks and move around, as objects do not behave in the same way they do on Earth. Not to worry though because due to our patented Bone Restore technology rockets, humans will still be able to live as if they would be around gravity.

Concern 2: Radiation

Space is filled with high-energy particles that can be harmful to humans. These particles can damage DNA and other cellular structures, and increase the risk of cancer and other diseases. Thanks to the Radionite fabric that covers the interior of our spacecraft, we can maintain safety standards that are within those of the CDC in terms of radiation.

Concern 3: Meteoroids

Small rocks and debris pose a threat to spacecraft and humans in space. Even relatively small meteoroids can cause significant damage to spacecraft and other objects. We have fortified our spacecraft with our proprietary AstroMesh metal alloys that will help ensure foreign objects do not harm the exterior of the aircraft.

Concern 4: Temperature Extremes

The temperature in space can vary widely, from hundreds of degrees below freezing to hundreds of degrees above boiling. This can be a problem for spacecraft and humans, as extreme temperatures can damage equipment and systems. The way we have solved this issue is by partnering with other space agencies. We have put together Atmosynth radiators that will maintain human-like conditions in every aspect of your travels there. Your comfort is our top priority.
